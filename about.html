About Me

Hello! My name is Natalie Bloomston and I am a graduate student at the University of Wyoming. While I may be based in Wyoming right now, Mobile, Alabama is where I call home. Before moving out west, I attended Auburn University in Auburn, Alabama, where I majored in Psychology and minored in Human Development and Family Sciences. After graduating with my bachelor's in 2017, I came to UW to pursue my master's degree in Zoology and Physiology. My thesis research focused on decision making in female songbirds, involving investigations of the neural mechanisms that underlie the decision making process. In addition to my research, I had the opportunity to TA General Biology and Animal Biology labs, helping me to discover my interest in educational technology and research.

I have since completed my master's degree and am now an Education PhD student in the Learning, Design, and Technology program at UW. Currently, my research involves assessing methods of using educational technology (specifically, Socially mediated Augmented Reality lessons) to aid students' learning, retention, and application of foundational STEM concepts. Our team's goal with this research is to improve students' understanding of micro-scale phenomena and associated spatial and mathematical representations through collaborative AR learning environments.
